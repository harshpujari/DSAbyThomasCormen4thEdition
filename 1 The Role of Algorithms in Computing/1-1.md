1-1 Comparison of running times

Let T be the available time in microseconds.
We want the largest value of n such that

f(n) <= T.

The time units are converted as follows:

- 1 second = 10^6 microseconds
- 1 minute = 6 * 10^7 microseconds
- 1 hour = 3.6 * 10^9 microseconds
- 1 day = 8.64 * 10^10 microseconds
- 1 month = 2.592 * 10^12 microseconds (assuming 30 days)
- 1 year = 3.1536 * 10^13 microseconds
- 1 century = 3.1536 * 10^15 microseconds

For each function, we solve for n:

- lg n: n = 2^T
- sqrt(n): n = sqrt(T)
- n: n = T
- n lg n: solve n lg n <= T, so n is approximately T / lg T
- n^2: n = sqrt(T)
- n^3: n = T^(1/3)
- 2^n: n = lg T
- n!: largest n such that n! <= T

Here is the completed table:

| Function f(n) | 1 second | 1 minute | 1 hour | 1 day | 1 month | 1 year | 1 century |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| lg n | 2^10^6 | 2^6*10^7 | 2^3.6*10^9 | 2^8.64*10^10 | 2^2.592*10^12 | 2^3.1536*10^13 | 2^3.1536*10^15 |
| sqrt(n) | 10^3 | 7.7 * 10^3 | 6.0 * 10^4 | 2.94 * 10^5 | 1.61 * 10^6 | 5.61 * 10^6 | 5.61 * 10^7 |
| n | 10^6 | 6 * 10^7 | 3.6 * 10^9 | 8.64 * 10^10 | 2.592 * 10^12 | 3.1536 * 10^13 | 3.1536 * 10^15 |
| n lg n | 5.0 * 10^4 | 2.3 * 10^6 | 1.1 * 10^8 | 2.4 * 10^9 | 6.3 * 10^10 | 7.0 * 10^11 | 6.2 * 10^13 |
| n^2 | 10^3 | 7.7 * 10^3 | 6.0 * 10^4 | 2.94 * 10^5 | 1.61 * 10^6 | 5.61 * 10^6 | 5.61 * 10^7 |
| n^3 | 10^2 | 3.9 * 10^2 | 1.53 * 10^3 | 4.42 * 10^3 | 1.37 * 10^4 | 3.16 * 10^4 | 1.47 * 10^5 |
| 2^n | 19 | 25 | 31 | 36 | 41 | 45 | 51 |
| n! | 9 | 11 | 12 | 13 | 15 | 16 | 17 |

Explanation:
- For polynomial-time functions such as n, n^2, and n^3, the largest solvable input size grows smoothly with the available time.
- For exponential functions such as 2^n, the size grows very slowly because the function grows extremely fast.
- For factorial functions, the input size is small even for long times because n! grows faster than exponential functions.
